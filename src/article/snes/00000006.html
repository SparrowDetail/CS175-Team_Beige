The Top 10 Best Selling SNES Games!

Steven Short 11/1/2024

Some History

The Super Nintendo was graced with phenomenal console sales, with over 49.1 million units sold worldwide. This could be impart attributed to Nintendo's dedication to its game quality control. Nintendo introduced its seal of quality in 1985 to the American and European markets to help distinguish officially licensed games from pirated knock-offs flooding the market. Now, with great console sales comes great game sales. Without further adieu, lets get into the top 10 Super Nintendo Games by sales!

10. Donkey Kong Country 3: Dixie Kong's Double Trouble!

Scoring a spot at number 10, we have Donkey Kong Country 3: Dixie Kong's Double Trouble! with over 3.51 million gross sales! Released by Rare in 1996, this game is the third installment of the Donkey Kong Country series. You play as Dixie Kong and. her cousin, Kiddy Kong as they attempt to rescue the captured Donkey Kong and Diddy Kong from the dastardly King K. Rool. With nicely rendered "silicon graphics" and well thought out environments, this platformer is sure to be a blast.

9. Street Fighter II Turbo: Hyper Fighting

Our fighting game fans will be pleased to see Street Fighter II Turbo: Hyper Fighting sitting snugly at number 9, with 4.1 million gross sales. Released by Capcom in 1992, the Turbo edition to the Street Fighter II series implemented faster input speeds allowing players to react and move quicker. Though special moves required more precise timing to execute, this game quickly gained popularity with its release on the Super Nintendo bringing it from Cabinet to home.

8. Super Mario World 2: Yoshi's Island

This 1995 wacky, whimsical platformer follows Baby Mario as he works with Yoshi to to rescue Baby Luigi from the clutches of the Magikoopa Kamek. Grossing over 4.12 million sales, Super Mario World 2: Yoshi's Island introduced itself as a prequel to the popular Super Mario World title. This game served as a leading inspiration for Yoshi's Story released on the Nintendo 64.

7. The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past

The Legend of Zelda franchise has generated a sprawling fantasy universe that contains a number of beloved adventure games since 1986. The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, released in 1991, boasted 4.61 million in gross sales and introduced a unique dimensional twist on the traditional formula. Link must travel between two parallel dimensions, the light world and the dark world, to defeat the evil Demon King Ganon and rescue the descendants of seven sages. This game has been critically acclaimed as one of the best video games ever made and has seen remakes or ports on most Nintendo consoles, including the Game Boy Advance, 3DS, Wii/Wii U, and Nintendo Switch.

6. Donkey Kong Country 2: Diddy's Kong Quest

Now we have the second game in the Donkey Kong Country series, Donkey Kong Country 2: Diddy's Kong Quest. Released in 1995 and seeing 5.15 million gross sales, similar to Donkey Kong Country 3, you play as Diddy Kong and Dixie Kong as you rescue Donkey Kong from the villainous clutches of King K. Rool. This game is yet another win for Rare in the SNES platform game scene.

5. Street Fighter II: The World Warrior

For the fighting game fans that were happy to see the Turbo edition at our ninth place, coming in at spot number 5 we have Street Fighter II: The World Warrior. Released one year prior to Turbo in 1991 with 6.3 million gross sales, this game was the direct successor to the first Street Fighter. Here we have the first introduction of the combo system, special command-based moves, and a wider selection of playable characters. As mentioned, the input precision required to complete combos and special moves was increased in Street Fighter Turbo: Hyper Fighter.

4. Super Mario Kart

The first game in the iconic Mario Kart series, Super Mario Kart was released in 1992 and saw 8.76 million in gross sales! Acting as Nintendo's competitor to the racing game market, this game, apposed to the single player focused standard of the time, was designed for two players with intuitive "pick up and play" controls. Similar to The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past, Super Mario Kart saw several ports on systems including the Wii/Wii U, 3DS, and Nintendo Switch.

3. Donkey Kong Country

This should be a familiar face! The first game of the Donkey Kong Country series, released in 1994 with 9.3 million gross sales! This classic game follows Donkey Kong and his nephew, Diddy Kong, as they attempt to rescue their banana hoard from, you guessed it, King K. Rool. This game really set the tempo for the success of the series and, as you can see with three games making this list, has really earned its place in the top three top grossing Super Nintendo Games!

2. Super Mario All-Stars

Released in 1993 and grossing 10.55 million in sales, earning our number two spot is Super Mario All-Stars. This game is a fantastic package containing remakes of the classic NES titles, including the original Super Mario Bros., exclusive Super Mario Bros.: Lost Levels, Super Mario Bros. 2, and Super Mario Bros. 3. This game really was the gift that kept giving, and, with so many prestigious games grouped in one convenient cartridge, its no wonder this game managed to become one of the highest grossing games on the Super Nintendo.

1. Super Mario World

Last, but certainly not least, we have a game that almost everyone who owned a Super Nintendo had in their collection. Released in Japan as Super Mario Bros. 4, but better known in North America as Super Mario World, we were blessed with this instant classic in 1990 with a whopping 20.61 million gross sales. Following a similar formula to the previous Super Mario Bros. games, you play as Mario as he traverses Dinosaur Land to rescue Princess Peach from Bowser and his Koopalings. Hosting beautifully enhanced visuals and loose feeling gameplay mechanics with a variety of secrets and extra zones to discover. This title truly deserves its place on the living room bookshelf!
